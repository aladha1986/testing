Executive Summary

The Q3 2025 financial statements are substantially accurate and internally consistent. All primary financial statement totals tie, all schedule rollforwards balance, and all note disclosures cross-reference correctly to the face statements. The three issues identified are low-severity presentation and rounding items — none represent a financial misstatement that affects reported net income, total assets, or shareholders’ equity. Seven analytical observations are flagged for management awareness, primarily relating to liquidity posture, FX exposure, G&A scaling, and one disclosure clarity item in the subsequent events note.

1

Issue 1 — Note 12 Working Capital Mixes Operating and Investing Items (Presentation)

Note 12 is titled “Changes in Non-Cash Working Capital” but combines both operating WC changes (AR, Inventory, Prepaid, AP) and the investing WC line (AP related to investing activities) in the same table. Summing only the four operating line items produces a result that does not match the operating WC total on the cash flow statement, because the investing WC of $2,998K (9M) and $(39)K (Q3) must be excluded. The cash flow statement correctly separates these, but Note 12 as presented could mislead a reader.

Note 12 operating-only sum (9M): $(5,926)K CF operating WC reported: $(2,928)K Unexplained gap: $2,998K = investing WC line Severity: Presentation only — no financial impact

Recommendation: Add a clear sub-total separating operating vs. investing WC in Note 12, or cross-reference the investing line to the CF statement to avoid reader confusion.

2

Issue 2 — $1K Rounding Discrepancy in Share Issuance Gross Proceeds (Note 7)

Note 7 states 7,555,500 shares were issued at $3.35 per share for gross proceeds of $25,310K. However, 7,555,500 × $3.35 = $25,310,925, which rounds to $25,311K, not $25,310K. As a result, the disclosed net proceeds ($25,310K − $1,817K = $23,493K) are understated by $1K versus the mathematically correct figure of $23,494K.

7,555,500 × $3.35 = $25,310,925 Rounds to: $25,311K Reported: $25,310K (off by $925) Net proceeds impact: $23,493K vs $23,494K

Recommendation: Confirm with underwriters whether gross proceeds were exactly $25,310,000 (implying a fractional offering price or contractual rounding). If $25,311K is correct, update the footnote. Difference is immaterial but should be documented.

3

Issue 3 — Subsequent Event Note Ambiguous on Replaced Facility Structure (Note 15)

Note 15 states the new $50,000K revolving facility “replaces the Company’s previous $30,000 credit facility.” However, Note 6 shows the replaced facility consists of two instruments: a $20,000K term loan and a $10,000K revolving loan (combined: $30,000K). Calling it a single “$30,000 credit facility” is technically accurate in aggregate but could mislead a reader unfamiliar with Note 6.

Term Loan: $20,000K Revolving Loan: $10,000K Combined: $30,000K (ties to Note 15) Severity: Disclosure clarity only

Recommendation: For Q4, rephrase as “replaces the Company’s previous ATB Financial credit facilities comprising a $20,000K term loan and $10,000K revolving facility.”

Flag 1 — Liquidity Tightening: Cash at $1,501K

Cash declined $4,262K YTD to $1,501K despite generating $10,715K from operations. The outflow is driven by $40,208K in CapEx for tower fleet expansion. The Company is materially reliant on its new $50,000K facility announced October 6 (Note 15). As at Q3, the revolving facility was already 64.7% utilized ($6,474K of $10,000K). Monitoring going-concern indicators is appropriate if operating cash flow does not scale with the fleet.

Flag 2 — Volatile AOCI / FX Translation Swings

Q3 2025 recorded a $749K FX gain in OCI, yet the 9M total is a $(750)K loss. This implies H1 2025 absorbed approximately $(1,499)K in FX translation losses — a significant intra-year swing. As US operations scale (now $42,616K of PP&E, 54.5% of total), USD/CAD volatility will increasingly affect reported book value.

Flag 3 — Gross Margin Stability: 63.3–63.5% (Positive)

Gross margin is healthy and consistent: 63.5% in Q3, 63.3% YTD, up from 59.2% in Q3 2024 (+430 bps). The expansion of the US business has not compressed margins, suggesting strong pricing discipline and operating leverage. This positive trend is worth highlighting in investor communications.

Q3 2025 GM: 63.5% 9M 2025 GM: 63.3% Q3 2024 GM: 59.2% YoY improvement: +430 bps

Flag 4 — Prepaid Expenses & Deposits Up $1,829K YTD (No Disclosure)

Combined current and non-current prepaid/deposits grew from $1,016K (Dec 2024) to $2,845K (Sep 2025), an increase of $1,829K (+180%). No explanatory note is provided. Given the rapid US expansion, this likely reflects facility security deposits or supplier advance payments, but disclosure of the nature of the increase would improve transparency.

Dec 2024: $1,016K Sep 2025: $2,845K (+$1,829K, +180%) No explanatory note provided

Flag 7 — G&A Growing Faster Than Revenue: 42.5% of Revenue vs 35.7% Prior Year
